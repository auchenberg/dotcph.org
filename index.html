dotcph

dotcph is an initiative to buy and secure the cph gTLD to represent the danish city Copenhagen on the world wide web, also known as the internet.

Tweet

Back in 2008, the organization managing the global domain system, ICANN, decided to open the internet up for a new breed of global top level domains, also known as gTLDs.

These gTLDs are the next generation of domains that will be used by the worlds population to access the future generation of websites and applications on the internet.

Today many oher european cities have gotten their own gTLD, like .berlin, .paris and .london, which, among many things, are used to create a close relationship between the cities and the owners of the domains, but right now Copenhagen is missing out.

We should change this.

Copenhagen has a vibraint creative culture, a strong and well-known design tradition, and a flourishing tech startup scene, so all the right elements are in place for us to buy and secure the cph gTLD,
but we need your help.

Let's make it happen

We are currently gathering an overview of the collective interest in the cph gTLD, by reaching out to companies, organizations and other stakeholders.

if you are interested in making the cph gTLD reality, please subscribe to the newsletter, and help us share this message on social media like Facebook and Twitter, as we need to build momentum amoung companies, organizations and the city of Copenhagen, to make it happen.
